Yet more Pokémon

By Katt | July 17, 2010

Yet more Pokémon doodles!  You know, sometimes I wonder why people with a ton of talent use it on things like Pokémon fan art or replicating Lady Gaga videos, rather than original creations.  But I figure they’re probably creating original stuff too, and besides, I guess I’m one of those people.

First we have the Bellsprout family.  For some reason I associate them with telephones.  Ma Bell?  Bellsprout looks like a candlestick phone to me.  The first two doodles here aren’t any good, but I like Victreebel.

I’ll preface the next one by saying: I’m not into steampunk.  I kind of like the idea of old-timey intepretations of modern objects, but there’s a lot of junk out there that someone spraypainted bronze and glued a bunch of gears to.  And steampunk is the kind of thing that people are into.  The serious, all-out connotation of into.  If you go to the store wearing a bracelet with gears on it, people assume you’ve got a drawerful of goggles and a BlackBerry retrofitted with tiny clacky typewriter keys.  People either love steampunk or hate it, and since I don’t love it, I’m supposed to hate it.  But I don’t, so here’s steampunk Porygon, complete with hilariously nonfunctional gears:

The below isn’t a very clear picture, either in the drawing or the scanning.  But the gist of it is: in this day and age, who writes checks at the grocery store?  Slowpoke, that’s who.

If you look closely you can see Bulbasaur getting impatient.  I’m not sure what’s on the right of Charmeleon’s head.  I think it’s part of some other doodle that I started and thought better of.

Slowbro’s leading leg gave me a lot of trouble.  The bottom attempt is not much better.

And finally Koffing, the only doodle of the bunch in color.  He’s farting in a tub.  I’m not sure there’s anything I can add that could make this less juvenile.

That’s all for now.  Even if I don’t do the Pokémonathon, I’ll probably have some higher-quality Pokémon drawings at some point in the future.  And I’ll get better at scanning.
