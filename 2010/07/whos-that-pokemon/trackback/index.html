Who’s that Pokémon?

By Katt | July 16, 2010

Here is the first of potentially many slightly embarrassing revelations: I really like Pokémon. And not because of nostalgia or camp value. Well, partly because of camp value. The games are well-designed and fun; there’s a lot to do, and they’re easy without being dull or tedious.

And there are all these weird, interesting critters. Not that they’re all brilliant, but I would love to be a fly on the wall at a Pokémon design team meeting. I imagine a scenario where they’re all locked in a room late at, and the boss won’t let them out until they’ve got 30 new Pokémon, and he’s certainly not springing for pizza.

Anyway, they’re fun to draw. I’ve really wanted to enter the Pokémonathon for over a year now. Two things got in my way: first, it would mean drawing a lot of Pokémon I didn’t particularly like, and second, some of those artists in the Pokémonathon are good. I figured I couldn’t measure up.

But I thought about it anyway, and I doodled. I doodled a lot. Here are some of the doodles I like, from maybe a year ago:

Fairly simplistic. I wonder if “drawing everyone with doofy expressions” is a legitimate style.

The Bulbasaur evolutionary line is my favorite. These guys aren’t perfect, but they amuse me:

I also like this Charmander and Charmeleon. If you’ve ever watched the cartoon (okay, fine, I did, but I swear that was purely for camp value), you’ll recall that Ash’s Charmander was kind of a wuss, and then he evolved into a surly Charmeleon. I like to assume that those personalities are species-wide, which I guess makes me a char-ist.

I hate Clefairy. I like Jigglypuff.

One day I’m going to be pitching ideas to some big important client, and they’re going to look up my name and discover that I once drew a picture of Wigglytuff on the toilet, and they will hire someone else.

None of these are Pokémonathon quality, but I may enter at some point – it’d be a good challenge. At any rate, drawing Pokémon is good practice, both for technique and for trying out different styles. I’ve got a few more, which I’ll post soon.
