]]> /2011/01/semi-tough-ladyz-4ever/feed/ 0 Monster mash /2010/12/monster-mash/ /2010/12/monster-mash/#comments Sun, 19 Dec 2010 17:00:06 +0000 Katt /?p=229 Because ’tis the season, here’s a special holiday comic:

I didn’t specify which holiday.

I drew this pretty hastily around Halloween, when our computers got stolen and I couldn’t scan it. I like it enough to share off-season.

The thing about comics that I’ve discovered since starting this blog is that it can be a challenge to make things visually legible. It’s like learning a new language with its own grammar and vocabulary. Other forms of art and writing can leave room for interpretation, but if you’re ambiguous in a comic you risk losing the punchline. When I drew this, I intended Ninja Turtle Girl to be yelling for her offscreen mom to help her with the scary lady, but afterwards I realized it could also (and more likely) be read as the scary lady being her mom, which I like a little better.

Bravest Ever will be on a short vacation; I’ll be back after Christmas. Enjoy whatever excuse to eat candy you celebrate!

]]> /2010/12/monster-mash/feed/ 0 I bet you also like clowns /2010/10/i-bet-you-also-like-clowns/ /2010/10/i-bet-you-also-like-clowns/#comments Thu, 21 Oct 2010 16:00:26 +0000 Katt /?p=169 I drew this retro-dorky cowboy kid when I drew yesterday’s husky boys. See, I was thinking about husky sizing being a relic of the past, like how “Western” used to be a popular movie genre, and how clowns used to be entertaining and not terrifying.

Turns out slim/regular/husky kids’ sizes still exist, and I’ve been wearing grown-up sizes for years, so it’s a thing of my childhood that I’d mistaken for a thing of the past. I looked into it, and Sears still sells slim and husky. So does Gap Kids. Abercrombie Kids only sells slim and regular, because everyone knows only cool kids wear Abercrombie, and fat kids can’t be cool. Kids are freaking mean.

But you can’t trust kids’ judgment. It’s all that TV they watch, you know.

I’ve been drawing these in a shiny new Whitelines notebook. I’m on the fence about it. I thought the paper would be white with printed white lines, in a slightly brighter white or a slightly glossy ink. Instead, the negative space is printed on, and it’s very slightly gray. I really like having the grid as a reference without it being distracting, but the lines don’t actually go completely away when scanned, which is disappointing. You still have to futz with the brightness and contrast levels, but it’s a little easier. If you look closely you can see the lines in the above picture, in addition to my lazy photoshopping. Trying to get the background of my scans to be perfectly white is still a challenge for me.
