Non-Skerple Friday: SkerpsCenter

By Katt | November 12, 2010

I did today’s Skerple Friday on the tablet instead, and I might do that from here on out. My Skerples are mostly dead, and I don’t have to futz around with the scanner.

I run at the gym a couple days after work, and the TV is always on either ESPN or the news. I just like having something moving to look at besides my reflection or the guy on the Airdyne, so whatever. Sometimes I’ll make up dialogue and pretend the news anchors are making fun of each others’ mothers. If someone’s covering the weather, I’ll pretend the correspondent in the anorak is cursing at the smug jerks in the studio who sent her there. It’s about as much fun as you can have at the gym; try it.

I’m always a little surprised with how obsessive the sports coverage is, though. Why do you need breaking news!! on ESPN?

Right? Let’s go to the scene of the sports.

The other thing I do is silently yell “SPORT!” in my head for every sports-related sound effect.

It’s kind of embarrassing how much I can entertain myself with a television on mute, now that I think of it.

A season of vengeance

By Katt | November 7, 2010

I have my Sketchbook Project sketchbook, and so far it is blank. I’ve got performance anxiety. The examples of past sketchbooks that I’ve seen are pretty good. Better than my finished stuff. My usual sketchbook contents aren’t so intricate, mostly scribbles and half-finished cartoon people and incomprehensible notes I took while I was on the phone with the dentist.

Of course, they probably put the coolest stuff on the front page anyway. That’s like thinking you have to be super-accomplished because everyone in your college’s alumni newsletter is doing fascinating stuff. I’ve never sent in my updates because they’re usually “yope, I’m still temping and I’ve almost conquered my apartment’s roach infestation!”

I’m thinking of doing a graphic novel-type thing for my sketchbook. This is pretty ambitious, and it’s gonna be a lot of work, and I should have already started. I have a vague story arc in my head; I need to flesh it out some.

And I’ve been working on what my protagonist will look like:

Not exactly like this, but pretty close. I try to draw her every day so I can get used to drawing her. I’m trying to adjust my style, too. The way I currently draw people isn’t very adaptable. I have a lot of trouble drawing convincing dudes, for example, and different ages.

Anyway, my story is called Vengeance Bus, and it’s going to be about exactly that. I’m excited about it.

Skerple Friday: Skerpamaphone

By Katt | October 22, 2010

Some days you wake up thinking about saxophones for whatever reason. And then you decide it’d be a good exercise to try and draw a few, with a couple clarinets thrown in for good measure.

I have never played the saxophone, so attempting to draw one from memory is a fool’s errand. I did, however, play oboe! Now that’s a fine instrument. The higher-end models come with many features and options, most of which are different ways to play F.

(Augh I am going to have to start remembering to look for that line that appears on the bottom of all my scans and start cropping it off. That’s not the edge of the paper; I don’t know what it is.)

I suspect F. Lorée or whoever invented the oboe didn’t think the ergonomics through, and then they had to add more alternate F keys as an afterthought.

Anyway I might have to borrow some woodwind instruments for sketching purposes. They’ve got tons of interesting detail.

I bet you also like clowns

By Katt | October 21, 2010

I drew this retro-dorky cowboy kid when I drew yesterday’s husky boys. See, I was thinking about husky sizing being a relic of the past, like how “Western” used to be a popular movie genre, and how clowns used to be entertaining and not terrifying.

Turns out slim/regular/husky kids’ sizes still exist, and I’ve been wearing grown-up sizes for years, so it’s a thing of my childhood that I’d mistaken for a thing of the past. I looked into it, and Sears still sells slim and husky. So does Gap Kids. Abercrombie Kids only sells slim and regular, because everyone knows only cool kids wear Abercrombie, and fat kids can’t be cool. Kids are freaking mean.

But you can’t trust kids’ judgment. It’s all that TV they watch, you know.

I’ve been drawing these in a shiny new Whitelines notebook. I’m on the fence about it. I thought the paper would be white with printed white lines, in a slightly brighter white or a slightly glossy ink. Instead, the negative space is printed on, and it’s very slightly gray. I really like having the grid as a reference without it being distracting, but the lines don’t actually go completely away when scanned, which is disappointing. You still have to futz with the brightness and contrast levels, but it’s a little easier. If you look closely you can see the lines in the above picture, in addition to my lazy photoshopping. Trying to get the background of my scans to be perfectly white is still a challenge for me.
