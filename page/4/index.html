Skerple Friday: I’m dying, Skerple

By Katt | October 8, 2010

It’s happening. My Skerples are drying out. They’re turning into Skerple corpses. (…skrerpses.) I may have to turn this into Sharpie Friday. I’m keeping the skerpses, though, and turning them into Christmas tree ornaments or something.

Anyway, I don’t know what all this is. I just woke up and started making lines.

I drew something I really didn’t like over on the right, and scratched-then-shopped it out. Then Bonus Cat showed up wondering when food was going to happen. The thinner black line is official Sharpie, aka fake Skerple.

And then I tried to draw the other guys from the Heathcliff cartoon. You know, the cats that weren’t Heathcliff. I’ve always wondered what sort of beef Heathcliff and Riff-Raff had such that they were never in the cartoon together. Riff-Raff was a lot better.

What is it with cartoon dudes being short and cartoon ladies being twice as tall? That always bugged me in the Flintstones. (Also that Wilma and Betty had identical bodies, and their waists were smaller around than Pebbles’ arm.) My best guess is that you can caricature a guy’s shortness all you want, but women have to be proportional enough to be hott. So, um, why exactly does a female cat in a kids’ cartoon have to be hott?

Also, I forgot that Cleo’s hair goes up, not out. The 80s are weird.

These guys were like the ambassadors who spoke to both Heathcliff and Riff-Raff. Maybe they delivered packages. Hector (the BORING one!) is also supposed to have bigger hair, and, hilariously, a skinny tie. I got Wordsworth’s headphones all wrong, but he found them in a junkyard.

Skerple Friday: 20 Skerple Team Borax

By Katt | October 1, 2010

A hasty one because I seem to be running late and not really awake yet. I haven’t been drawing a ton lately because I just started knitting this great sweater, and I never get totally into knitting, so might as well ride the enthusiasm while I can.

They redesigned the Borax box. It used to be cheap-looking and awesome, and now it is all dumb. (I am too lazy to find a picture of the old box.) I figure if you’re going to redesign it, make it louder, make the word BORAX even bigger, and add lots of exclamation points. Right?

YEAH BORAX. Or how about add some cartoon endorsement and rhyming appeal in there?

Zorak doesn’t look mean enough, but that’s because he’s excited to wash his sock.

Or: say it in Helvetica.

There’s this trend I’ve noticed where package designers who think they are TOTALLY BRILLIANT decide to just make the box entirely white and fill it with five-hundred-point Helvetica, line-breaking every two letters. Then they charge you extra for the Gutes Design.

That’s because they had to pay both the designer and the guy who told the designer they have to fix the space between the A and the X.

Skerple Friday: Smells like Teen Skerple

By Katt | September 17, 2010

Last night I had a dream that Kate Beaton, whose work I love*, had some sort of technical difficulties that prevented any dialogue or captions from appearing on her comics, so she held a caption contest on Twitter. I think all the comics were about babies.

Then I fell back asleep, and between Bonus Cat chewing tape and biting my scalp, I had dreams about washing underwear. And for some reason when I woke up I drew this.

I’m not completely sure why I have so much trouble drawing two eyes that look in the same direction.

There’s probably three dozen anachronisms lovingly hand-skerpled into the above, so I’m just gonna say (1) I was never very good at history (2) it’s, um, steampunk. She has cogs on her corset or something. You can get away with a lot if you say that it’s steampunk.

Of course, I have no reservations about mentioning my underwear or washing them in the machine.

“Stank” might actually be some sort of hot cereal, in which case Ke$ha? is going to get a surprise in thirty to forty-five minutes, or whenever she remembers that she put something in the washing machine.

*and who, I like to imagine, would TOTES be my BFF if we ever met. Same with Allie Brosh. I have a feeling, though, most 18-34 year old women think the same thing, and that if you are clever and talented and have a large fanbase, you probably have enough BFFs and wannabe BFFs for a while. No stalksies!
